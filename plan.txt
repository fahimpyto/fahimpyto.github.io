---

# 1. The Vision

Don't build **just a portfolio**.

Build your **professional home on the internet**.

When someone searches:

> **Fahim Ahmed**
> or
> **fahimpyto**

they should find one website that answers everything:

* Who are you?
* What do you build?
* What skills do you have?
* What have you written?
* What projects have you completed?
* What open-source software have you published?
* What are you currently learning?
* How can someone hire or contact you?

Think of your website as a combination of:

* Portfolio
* Blog
* Knowledge Base
* Resume
* Case Studies
* Digital Garden
* Personal Brand

# 5. Design Philosophy

Don't copy typical developer portfolios.

Avoid:

* Hacker terminal everywhere
* Neon overload
* Endless scrolling animations
* Busy dashboards

Instead, aim for:

* Modern
* Elegant
* Spacious
* Premium
* Professional
* Content-first
* Image-rich

Think of a blend between:

* Apple
* Stripe
* Vercel
* Linear
* GitHub Docs

Clean interfaces age much better than flashy ones.

Keeping content separate from components makes the site easy to maintain.

---

# 23. Content Workflow

Every new blog post is an MDX file.

Every new project is a folder with:

* Markdown
* Images
* Assets

Every new achievement is a single entry in a data file.

This means adding content is quick and consistent.

# 25. Content Strategy

Aim to publish consistently rather than frequently.

For example:

* 1 project update each month
* 2–4 technical articles each month
* Update achievements whenever you reach a milestone
* Refresh the "Now" section every few months

Over time, your website becomes a record of your professional growth.

---

# Final Advice

Treat **fahimpyto** as a **product**, not just a website.

Before writing code, spend time designing the experience in Figma or Penpot. Define your colors, typography, buttons, cards, spacing, and layouts. Then build those designs in Astro.

The result will be a fast, free, image-rich, SEO-friendly website that showcases your projects, technical writing, open-source work like **metascrub**, and evolving expertise in Python, automation, data science, Django, SEO, AI, and machine learning. Most importantly, it will be structured to grow with your career instead of needing to be rebuilt every year.
